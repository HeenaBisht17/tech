Women in Technology:

"Breaking Barriers, Building the Future"


Women have been vital contributors to the field of technology—from the early days of computing to the forefront of today's innovations. They laid the groundwork for modern computing.




She codes, she leads, she inspires:Ada Lovelace

Ada Lovelace (1815–1852) was an English mathematician and writer, the world's first computer programmer,widely recognized as the world's first computer programmer. She is best known for her work with Charles Babbage on his proposed mechanical general-purpose computer, the Analytical Engine.
In 1843, she translated an Italian paper on the engine and added extensive notes, three times longer than the original.
Her insights laid the foundation for modern computing.
Ada Lovelace remains a symbol of women’s lasting impact in technology.

Other Trailblazing Women in Technology

Reshma Saujani

Known For: Founding Girls Who Code, which has reached millions of girls worldwide. Mission: Closing the gender gap in tech through education and empowerment.

Margaret Hamilton

Known For: Leading NASA's software engineering team for the Apollo 11 moon landing. Legacy: Coined the term “software engineering.”

Grace Hopper

Known For: Inventing the first compiler and helping develop COBOL, a foundational programming language. Legacy: Nicknamed “Amazing Grace,” she revolutionized how humans interact with machines.

Tessy Thomas

Missile Technology / DRDO (Defence Research and Development Organisation),First woman to lead a missile project in India. Played a pivotal role in the development of the Agni-IV and Agni-V ballistic missiles.

Ritu Karidha

pace Science / ISRO (Indian Space Research Organisation) Known As: “Rocket Woman of India” Played a leading role in Chandrayaan-2 as Mission Director. Deputy Operations Director of India’s Mars Orbiter Mission (Mangalyaan) in 2013.

Gagandeep Kang

Gagandeep Kang is a renowned Indian scientist known for her work in public health and vaccines.Indian virologist who led COVID-19 vaccine development efforts.
